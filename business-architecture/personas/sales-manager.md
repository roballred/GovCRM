# Persona: Sales Manager

**Validation Status: [UNVERIFIED] Assumed** — synthesized from vendor market research and common role patterns; would move to Validated through interviews with real first-line sales managers.

## Role Type
Internal — Sales leadership (first-line)

## Who They Are
The Sales Manager leads a team of reps and owns the team number. They run pipeline reviews, coach reps on deals, reassign leads and accounts, and submit the forecast upward. They are the primary consumer of pipeline views and the primary enforcer of CRM discipline.

## Goals
- Submit a forecast they can defend, built on pipeline data they trust
- Spot at-risk deals early enough to coach or intervene
- Balance lead and account distribution across the team
- Run pipeline reviews from live data instead of spreadsheets assembled the night before
- Ramp new reps quickly using a defined, visible sales process

## Pain Points
- Pipeline stages that mean different things to different reps
- Forecast calls based on rep optimism rather than evidence
- No early-warning signal for stalled or slipping deals
- Coaching from anecdote because activity data is incomplete
- Reports that require an analyst every time the question changes

## Critical Insight
The manager's real product is the forecast, and its accuracy is set by rep behavior weeks earlier. Capabilities that standardize stage definitions and surface deal risk automatically do more for forecast quality than any forecasting feature itself.
