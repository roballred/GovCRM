# Persona: Support Manager

**Validation Status: [UNVERIFIED] Assumed** — synthesized from vendor market research and common role patterns; would move to Validated through interviews with real support or service delivery managers.

## Role Type
Internal — Customer Service leadership

## Who They Are
The Support Manager runs the service team: staffing the queue, setting SLA policy, monitoring backlog and satisfaction, and handling escalations. They answer for service quality to executives and for workload fairness to agents.

## Goals
- Keep SLA attainment and customer satisfaction visibly on target
- Balance queue load across agents and channels
- Identify recurring issue patterns and drive them to root-cause fixes
- Grow the knowledge base so common issues stop reaching senior agents
- Give sales and account teams early warning on at-risk customers

## Pain Points
- Backlog and SLA breaches discovered after the fact, not predicted
- Case categories too messy to reveal what actually drives volume
- Agent performance metrics that reward speed over resolution quality
- Escalations arriving with no context and no ownership trail
- Satisfaction data collected but never linked to specific cases or agents

## Critical Insight
The Support Manager's leverage is in patterns, not individual cases. Categorization discipline and feedback linkage — the least glamorous capabilities — are what turn a ticket queue into a product-improvement engine.
