# Persona: Account Manager

**Validation Status: [UNVERIFIED] Assumed** — synthesized from vendor market research and common role patterns; would move to Validated through interviews with real account managers or customer success managers.

## Role Type
Internal — Sales / Customer Success (post-sale)

## Who They Are
The Account Manager owns ongoing customer relationships after the initial sale: renewals, upsells, cross-sells, and overall account health. They work a book of existing accounts rather than net-new pipeline, and they depend on seeing the whole relationship — purchases, open support cases, recent complaints, active projects — in one place.

## Goals
- See complete account health (revenue, activity, open cases, sentiment) at a glance
- Never be surprised in a renewal conversation by an unresolved support issue
- Identify expansion opportunities from usage and relationship signals
- Maintain relationship continuity when contacts change roles or leave
- Plan and log recurring touchpoints across a large book of accounts

## Pain Points
- Sales and support data live in different systems or silos of the same system
- Account hierarchies (parent/subsidiary) poorly modeled, splitting the true picture
- Renewal dates and commitments tracked in personal spreadsheets
- Contact churn at customers goes unnoticed until email bounces
- Activity history fragmented across the reps who touched the account before them

## Critical Insight
The Account Manager is the persona for whom "360-degree customer view" is not a slogan but the daily job. They are the strongest argument for cases, deals, contacts, and activities sharing one data model — every silo directly costs them renewal revenue.
