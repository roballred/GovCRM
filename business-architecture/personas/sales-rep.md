# Persona: Sales Representative

**Validation Status: [UNVERIFIED] Assumed** — synthesized from vendor market research and common role patterns; would move to Validated through interviews with real quota-carrying reps.

## Role Type
Internal — Sales / Revenue

## Who They Are
The Sales Representative is a quota-carrying individual contributor who prospects, qualifies leads, works opportunities through the pipeline, and closes deals. They live in email, calls, and meetings; the CRM is where they are told to record all of it. Their compensation depends on closed revenue, not on CRM hygiene.

## Goals
- Spend the maximum share of their day selling, not doing data entry
- Know instantly which leads and deals deserve attention right now
- Have full context on a contact (history, emails, open issues) before every call
- Generate accurate quotes quickly without chasing product and pricing data
- Hit quota and see clearly where they stand against it

## Pain Points
- Duplicate and manual data entry between email, calendar, and CRM
- Stale or duplicate contact records that make outreach embarrassing
- No visibility into marketing touches or support tickets on their own accounts
- Forecasting rituals that demand data cleanup at the worst possible time
- Tools that work at a desk but not on the road

## Critical Insight
The rep only maintains CRM data when the CRM gives back more time than it takes. Every capability that serves managers and analysts (forecasts, reports, dashboards) is downstream of a rep deciding data entry is worth it — automatic capture, not policy, is what makes the whole system truthful.
