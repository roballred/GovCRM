# Persona: Marketing Manager

**Validation Status: [UNVERIFIED] Assumed** — synthesized from vendor market research and common role patterns; would move to Validated through interviews with real demand-generation or marketing operations managers.

## Role Type
Internal — Marketing

## Who They Are
The Marketing Manager plans and runs campaigns that generate and nurture leads for the sales team. They own email programs, landing pages and forms, segmentation, and the marketing-to-sales handoff. They are measured on lead volume and quality, and increasingly on pipeline influenced.

## Goals
- Capture leads from web, events, and content directly into the CRM with source attribution
- Segment audiences precisely from CRM data without exporting to spreadsheets
- Nurture leads automatically until they are sales-ready, then hand off cleanly
- Score leads so sales works the best ones first — and trusts the score
- Show which campaigns actually produced pipeline

## Pain Points
- Leads passed to sales too early die; passed too late they go cold
- Sales ignores lead scores they don't understand or trust
- Segmentation limited by dirty, duplicate, or sparse contact data
- Campaign membership and outcomes not linked back to revenue
- Every list pull requires an export, a spreadsheet, and a re-import

## Critical Insight
The marketing-to-sales handoff is a trust boundary, not a data transfer. Lead scoring only changes sales behavior when reps can see *why* a lead scored high — explainability in the score is worth more than accuracy in the model.
