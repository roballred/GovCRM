# Persona: Executive Sponsor

**Validation Status: [UNVERIFIED] Assumed** — synthesized from vendor market research and common role patterns; would move to Validated through interviews with real revenue executives (VP Sales, CRO, COO).

## Role Type
Internal — Executive (VP Sales / CRO / COO)

## Who They Are
The Executive Sponsor funds the CRM and answers to the board for revenue predictability. They rarely touch records; they consume dashboards, forecasts, and summaries, and they set the tone for whether the organization treats the CRM as the system of record or as bureaucratic overhead.

## Goals
- Trust the revenue number: pipeline, forecast, and bookings from one source
- See leading indicators (pipeline coverage, conversion, velocity) not just results
- Understand customer health across sales, service, and marketing in one view
- Justify the CRM investment with adoption and outcome evidence
- Make org-design and territory decisions from data, not anecdote

## Pain Points
- Competing numbers from sales, finance, and ops for the same question
- Dashboards that demand tool fluency they don't have time to build
- Discovering data-quality problems only when a board number proves wrong
- Adoption theater: license counts presented as usage
- Insight arriving as a monthly deck instead of a live view

## Critical Insight
The executive decides the CRM's fate on one criterion: whether they instinctively open it (or its dashboard) before asking a person. Plain-language, zero-training views for this persona are what convert the tool from a cost center into the system of record.

- `ar-dashboards`, `ar-sales-analytics`, `ar-ai-insights`
- `sfa-sales-forecasting`
- `cs-customer-feedback` (aggregate view)
- `pa-security-controls` (accountability for customer data)
